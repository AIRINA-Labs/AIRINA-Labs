AIRINA Labs · Training & Certifications

Short-form. Mathematically rigorous. Research-led.

Two-day to two-week programs in topological data analysis, interpretable AI, and the mathematical foundations of robust machine learning. Graded certificates. Bilingual delivery. Each cohort is small and instructor-dense.

Format In-person · Cotonou & partner sites Languages EN / FR Cohorts 8–18 participants

Our trainings are not an introduction to data science. They are short-form, mathematically deep, and built around the unit's active research programs — what we teach is what we publish.

Anchor programs · Launching first

Tier 1 — what we run now.

/01

TDA for Data Scientists

3 days · In-person · Bilingual EN/FR · Cohort ~12
Launching Q3 2026

Hands-on entry to topological data analysis for working data scientists. We build the GUDHI / Ripser / giotto-tda pipeline end to end — from point clouds, to filtrations, to persistence diagrams, to ML-ready features.

Audience

Data scientists, statisticians, and ML engineers from banks, MFIs, telcos, and financial institutions. AIMS-alum graduate students with quantitative backgrounds.

Prerequisites

Working Python (NumPy / pandas). Calculus and linear algebra. No prior topology required.

Outline

  1. Day 1. Point clouds to filtrations — Vietoris–Rips, Čech, alpha complexes. Persistent homology by hand and in code (GUDHI, Ripser).
  2. Day 2. Persistence diagrams, barcodes, landscapes — interpretation and the stability theorem. Vectorization for ML in giotto-tda.
  3. Day 3. TDA on real data — time series, networks, images. Graded final mini-project and defense.

/02

Interpretable ML for Regulated Industries

3 days · In-person · Bilingual EN/FR · Cohort ~12
Launching Q4 2026

For risk and analytics teams at banks, MFIs, and insurers. Interpretable-by-design models, post-hoc explanations and their failure modes, fairness audits under African data realities, and an introduction to neuro-symbolic methods.

Audience

Risk modellers, credit and fraud analysts, model validation teams, regulatory analytics at financial institutions.

Prerequisites

Working data-science Python; familiarity with logistic regression, random forests, gradient boosting.

Outline

  1. Day 1. Regulatory landscape (BCEAO, BCBS, EU AI Act); interpretability vs explainability; interpretable-by-design models — GAMs, monotonic gradient boosting, decision lists, rule extraction.
  2. Day 2. Post-hoc explanations — SHAP, LIME, counterfactuals — strengths, failure modes, and what NOT to claim. Fairness audits and disparate-impact analysis on African data.
  3. Day 3. Neuro-symbolic methods for decisioning. Graded final — model plus interpretability and fairness audit on a real dataset.

/03

Topological Foundations of Deep Learning

2 weeks · Residential, Cotonou · Cohort ~15
2027 summer school

A summer school on what topology has to say about deep learning — the manifold hypothesis, latent geometry, persistent homology of activations, differentiable persistence, and topology-aware regularization. Heavy on theory and code in equal measure.

Audience

Graduate students in mathematics / CS / statistics, industry research engineers, advanced ML practitioners.

Prerequisites

Solid deep-learning practice; comfort with measure-theoretic probability or algebraic topology (one or the other).

Outline

  1. Week 1. Manifold hypothesis; latent geometry of representations; persistent homology of activations; topological signatures of generalization and capacity.
  2. Week 2. Topological regularization; differentiable persistence and topological loss functions; robustness and OOD detection via topology. Research mini-project and final talk.

How we certify

The certificate has to mean something.

AIRINA does not issue certificates of attendance. Every training ends with a graded final — a TDA pipeline on a real dataset, an interpretability and fairness audit, a research mini-project — defended in front of AIRINA researchers. That is what makes the certificate worth listing on a CV.

Where appropriate, the longer programs (Tier 2 and the research school) are co-accredited with university partners so they carry academic weight beyond the AIRINA brand.

Training × research

One engine, two timescales.

Each training has a paid industry-consulting twin. A bank that sends two analysts to TDA for Data Scientists can then hire AIRINA on a six-week engagement applying TDA to its fraud-detection problem. The engagement generates the case study used in the next cohort. The published paper from that engagement gives the next training its credibility.

Top participants from short trainings are recruited into AIRINA research collaborations on the deeper themes — symbolic and interpretable AI, topological foundations of RL, quantum-topological AI for finance. The certificate program is the front door; the research arm is the back room. Same engine, different timescales.
